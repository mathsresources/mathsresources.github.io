Non-calculator.
55 marks.

Question 1

(4 marks)

To find the equation of a line you will need to find its gradient.

How can you find the gradient of a tangent to a curve?

Differentiate and substitute in \(x=\) to find the gradient of the tangent.

Next you just need to know a point the tangent passes through.

How can you find the \(y\)-coordinate which corresponds to \(x=2\)?

Substitute \(x=2\) into \(y=x^3-2x^2+5\) to find the corresponding \(y\)-coordinate.

Use that coordinate together with the gradient found and the straight line equation \(y-b=m(x-a)\).

\(y=4x-3\)

Question 2

(4 marks)

What are the properties of a perpendicular bisector?

Start by finding the gradient of line AB.

What then is the gradient of its perpendicular bisector?

Once you have the gradient perpendicular to line AB, find the midpoint of AB.

This coordinate and the perpendicular gradient can be used with \(y-b=m(x-a)\).

\(3y=-4x+41\) or \(y=-\dfrac{4}{3}x+\dfrac{41}{3}\)

Question 3

(4 marks)

One of the terms is not yet ready to be integrated.

Which one? How can it be rewritten?

\(\dfrac{12}{x^2}\) can be written as \(12x^{-2}\).

The rule for integrating is increase the power by one then divide by the new power.

What should the answer to an indefinite integration include?

Dividing by \(\dfrac{3}{2}\) is the same as multiplying by \(\dfrac{2}{3}\).

Remember to simplify each part as much as possible and include "\(+\mathrm{C}\)".

\(-12x^{-1}+\dfrac{2}{3}x^{\frac{3}{2}}+\mathrm{C}\)

Question 4

(3 marks)

What are the three log laws?

Which must be applied first here?

Use the power law \(y\log_ax=\log_ax^y\) to remove the \(2\) from the front of the first log.

Next, what happens when to logs are added?

Remember that \(3^{-1}=\dfrac{1}{3}\).

What therefore is the value of \(\log_3{\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)}\)?

\(-1\)

Question 5

(2 marks)

This is a graph transformations question.

What does the transformation \(f(-x)\dots\) do?

What does the transformation \(f(\dots)+3\) do?

Write down the three coordinates given on the graph of \(y=f(x)\).

\(f(-x)\dots\) "flips" the \(x\)-coordinates.

\(f(\dots)+3\) adds \(3\) to the \(y\)-coordinates.

Write down the three transformed coordinates.

The three coordinates are \((-4,3\), \((0,6)\) and \((2,3)\).

Use them to sketch a graph which is "flipped horizontally" and shifted up \(3\).

Question 6

(4 marks)

The diagram shows angle \(q\) but the questions ask about \(2Q\).

How can \(\sin{2q}\) and \(\cos{2q}\) be expanded?

The length of the right-angled triangle's hypotenuse will be needed in this question.

Use \(\sin{2q}=2\sin{q}\cos{q}\) and \(\cos{2q}=2\cos^2{q}-1\).

The length of the hypotenuse is \(\sqrt{5^2+1^2}\).

From the triangle, write down the values of \(\sin{q}\) and \(\cos{q}\).

Substitute the values of \(\sin{q}=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{26}}\) and \(\cos{q}=\dfrac{5}{\sqrt{26}}\).

Then simplify.

\(\sin{2q}=\dfrac{5}{13}\) and \(\cos{2q}=\dfrac{12}{13}\)

(3 marks)

How can \(\sin{(2q-r)}\) be expanded?

Use \(\sin{(2q-r)}=\sin{2q}\cos{r}-\cos{2q}\sin{r}\).

The values of \(\sin{2q}\) and \(\cos{2q}\) have already been found in part (a).

What are the values of \(\sin{r}\) and \(\cos{r}\)?

Substitute \(\sin{r}=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{17}}\) and \(\cos{r}=\dfrac{4}{\sqrt{17}}\) into the expanded form.

\(\dfrac{8}{13\sqrt{17}}\)
